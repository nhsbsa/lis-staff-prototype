Challenge a penalty charge

There are two ways you can challenge a Penalty Charge Notice:

If you think there has been a mistake

You can challenge a Penalty Charge Notice if:

  • you were entitled to claim free NHS prescriptions or NHS dental treatment (you can check your entitlement here)
  • you had a valid NHS medical exemption certificate or NHS maternity exemption certificate
  • you had a valid prescription prepayment certificate (PPC)

How do mistakes occur?

  • Out of date personal details
    You must make sure that your GP surgery, dental practice, benefits organisations and the NHS Business Services Authority have your correct name and address. If these details are not kept up to date you may get a Penalty Charge Notice, even if you are entitled to free prescriptions or dental treatment.
  • You ticked the wrong box

How to challenge your Penalty Charge Notice

If you can confirm that you were entitled to claim free prescriptions or dental treatment between the dates on your Penalty Charge Notice you must contact us.

Providing proof

Once you have contacted us, we will ask you to provide documentation to prove your exemption status.

If you are unable to challenge your Penalty Charge Notice

If you were not entitled to claim free NHS prescriptions or dental treatment during the dates on your Penalty Charge Notice, you must pay the charges listed on the letter. Pay your Penalty Charge Notice here.

Don’t delay. You can pay in instalments – but you will need to pay the first instalment straight away.
