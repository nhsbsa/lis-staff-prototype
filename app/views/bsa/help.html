Check who gets free or reduced prescriptions or dental treatment

Use our online eligibility checker to find out whether you can claim free or reduced NHS prescriptions or dental treatment.

Don't assume you're automatically covered

Clcik the group that matches your situation, to find out what you need to do to claim free or reduced NHS prescriptions or dental treatment:

Low income

If you have a low income you cannot automatically claim free or reduced NHS prescriptions or dental treatment. You must apply to the NHS Low Income Scheme by completing an HC1 form.

Apply to the NHS Low Income Scheme

After an assessment, depending on your financial circumstances you might be awarded a HC2 certificate or HC3 certificate.

HC2 certificate

This certicate entitles you to

  • free NHS prescriptions, NHS dental treatment, NHS sight tests and NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • help with the cost of glasses or contact lenses and help with the cost of travelling to receive NHS treatment

HC3 certificate

This certificate does not entitle you to free prescriptions, but it does entitle you to:

  • help with the cost of NHS dental treatment, NHS sight tests, travelling to receive NHS treatment, NHS wigs and fabric supports

Your certificate will tell you how much help you will get.

At the pharmacy or dental practice

When ticking the box on the back of the prescription form or dental declaration form, it is your responsibility to check that your HC2 or HC3 certificate is still in date and has not expired.

If your personal details change

If your name or address changes after your NHS maternity exemption certificate has been issued, you must let the NHSBSA know. Contact us here.

Benefits and tax credits

Benefits

If you or your partner receive any of the following benefits, or you’re under 20 and the dependent of someone receiving any of these, you are entitled to claim free NHS prescriptions and NHS dental treatment:

  • Income support
  • Income-based Job Seekers Allowance (JSA)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Pension Credit Guarantee Credit
  • Pension Credit Guarantee Credit with Savings Credit
  • Universal Credit – with Universal Credit you can claim free prescriptions if your earnings during an assessment period were £435 or less, or £935 or less with a credit for either a child or a limited capability for work

At the pharmacy or dental practice

When ticking the box on the back of the prescription form or dental declaration form, it is your responsibility to check that your benefits status still entitles you to claim. A change in your circumstances may mean you no longer get benefits.

If pharmacy or dental practice staff ask for proof of your eligibility, show one of the following: Income Support award letter, Job Seekers Allowance award letter, Employment and Support Allowance Award letter, Pension Credit award letter or Universal Credit statement.

If you are under 20 you will need to show your parent or guardian’s qualifying benefit award letter that includes you as a dependent.

Find out more about benefits (GOV.UK website)

Tax credits

If you receive Tax Credits you are not automatically entitled to claim free NHS prescriptions and or dental treatment.

However, if your annual family income is £15,276 or less and you receive one of the following:

  • Child Tax Credit
  • Child Tax Credit and Working Tax Credit paid together
  • Working Tax Credit including a disability element

HM Revenue and Customs will inform us and we will send you an NHS tax credit exemption certificate. You don’t need to apply.

Once you have a valid NHS tax credit exemption certificate you can claim free NHS prescriptions and NHS dental treatment.

At the pharmacy

When ticking the box on the back of the prescription or dental declaration form, it is your responsibility to check that your Tax Credit exemption certificate is still in date and has not expired.

If pharmacy or dental practice staff ask for proof of your eligibility, show your Tax Credit exemption certificate or Tax Credit award notice.

Find out more about Tax Credits (GOV.UK website)

If your personal details change

If your name or address changes, you must let the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) know.

Pregnant

If you are pregnant or have given birth in the last 12 months you are not automatically entitled to claim free NHS prescriptions, you must apply for an NHS maternity exemption certificate.

Once you have a valid maternity exemption certificate you can claim free NHS prescriptions.

Maternity exemption certificates are valid for 12 months after your baby’s due date or date of birth. It is your responsibility to check that it is still in date and has not expired.

How to apply for an NHS maternity exemption certificate

At the pharmacy

When ticking the maternity exemption box on the back of the prescription form, it is your responsibility to check that your exemption certificate is still in date and has not expired.

If pharmacy staff ask for proof of your maternity exemption, show your NHS maternity exemption certificate.

If your personal details change

name or address changes after your NHS maternity exemption certificate has been issued, you must let the NHSBSA know. Contact us here.

Specific medical conditions

Specific medical conditions do not automatically entitle you to claim free NHS prescriptions. You must also have a valid NHS medical exemption certificate.

Medical exemption certificates are valid for 5 years. It is your responsibility to check that it is still in date and has not expired.

If you have any of the following conditions you can apply for a medical exemption certificate:

  • cancer – including treatment for the effects of cancer or the effects of cancer treatment
  • diabetes (type 1 or type 2) - if you need medication to keep blood sugar levels stable
  • diabetes insipidus and other forms of hypopituitarism
  • epilepsy – if you need continuous anticonvulsive therapy
  • a permanent fistula which needs continuous surgical dressing or an appliance – for example, caecostomy, colostomy, laryngostomy or ileostomy
  • hypoadrenalism, where specific substitution therapy is essential – for example, Addison’s Disease
  • hypoparathyroidism – which means your parathyroid glands produce too little parathyroid hormone
  • myasthenia gravis – where an autoimmune condition affects your nerves and muscles
  • myxoedema – for example, hypothyroidism which needs thyroid hormone replacement

How to apply for an NHS medical exemption certificate

At the pharmacy

When ticking the medical exemption box on the back of the prescription form, it is your responsibility to check that your exemption certificate is still in date and has not expired.

If pharmacy staff ask for proof of your medical exemption, show your NHS medical exemption certificate.

If your personal details change

If your name or address changes after your NHS medical exemption certificate has been issued, you must let the NHSBSA know. Contact us here.

Continuing physical disability

Once you have a valid medical exemption certificate you can claim free NHS prescriptions.

Medical exemption certificates are valid for 5 years. It is your responsibility to check that it is still in date and has not expired.

If you receive Incapacity Benefit, Disability Living Allowance, or Personal Independence Payment, this will not entitle to claim free NHS prescriptions. You must have a valid NHS medical exemption certificate.

How to apply for an NHS medical exemption certificate

At the pharmacy

When ticking the medical exemption box on the back of the prescription form, it is your responsibility to check that your exemption certificate is still in date and has not expired.

If pharmacy staff ask for proof of your medical exemption, show your NHS medical exemption certificate.

If your personal details change

If your name or address changes after your NHS medical exemption certificate has been issued, you must let the NHSBSA know. Contact us here.

War pension

If you receive a war pension and are under the age of 60, you can request a prescription exemption certificate from Veterans UK.

With this exemption certificate you can claim free NHS prescriptions, as long as the prescriptions are for your accepted disablement.

You may also be able to claim money back from Veterans UK for NHS dental treatment, if the treatment relates to your accepted disablement.

Contact Veterans UK:

  • Helpline: 0808 1914 218
  • Address: The Treatment Group, Veterans UK, Ministry of Defence, Norcross, Thornton, Cleveleys FY5 3WP
Find out more about prescription exemption certificates for war pensioners

At the pharmacy

If pharmacy staff ask for proof of your exemption, show your war pension exemption certificate.

If your name or address changes

If your name or address changes after your prescription exemption certificate has been issued, you need to let Veterans UK know. Contact them using the details above.

Under 16

If you are under 16 you can claim free NHS prescriptions and free NHS dental treatment.

At the pharmacy or dental practice

If pharmacy or dental practice staff ask for proof of your age, show your birth certificate, passport or medical card.

If your name or address changes

If your name or address changes make sure you have updated your personal details at your GP surgery and dental practice.

16, 17 or 18 and in full time education

If you are 16, 17 or 18 and in full time education you can claim free NHS prescriptions and free NHS dental treatment.

At the pharmacy or dental practice

If pharmacy or dental practice staff ask for proof, show your child benefits award letter, birth certificate, passport or medical card, and proof that you are a full time student.

If your name or address changes

If your name or address changes you must update your personal details at your GP surgery and dental practice.

Over 60

If you are over 60 you can claim free NHS prescriptions.

At the pharmacy

If pharmacy staff ask for proof of your age, show your birth certificate, passport, driving licence or medical card.

If your name or address changes

If your name or address changes you must update your personal details at your GP surgery

Ticking the right box at the pharmacy or dental practice

When you complete the back of a prescription form or a dental declaration form, it is your responsibility to make sure you tick the correct box.

If someone is collecting prescriptions on your behalf, make sure they know to tick the correct box on the back of the prescription. If dental staff are completing your dental form for you to sign, make sure they have ticked the right box for your eligibility.
