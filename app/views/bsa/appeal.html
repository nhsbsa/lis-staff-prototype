Challenge a penalty charge

How to challenge

There are two ways you can challenge an NHS Penalty Charge Notice. Choose the one that matches your situation:

You think there has been a mistake

You can challenge a Penalty Charge Notice for the following reasons:

  • you were entitled to claim free or reduced prescriptions or dental treatment. Check your entitlement here.
  • you had a valid medical exemption certificate or maternity exemption certificate. Check it had not expired.
  • you had a valid prescription prepayment certificate (PPC). Check it had not expired.

If you can confirm that you were entitled to free or reduced prescriptions or dental treatment between the dates on your Penalty Charge Notice, contact us as soon as possible.

How do mistakes occur?

  • Out of date personal details
    You must make sure that your GP surgery, dental practice, any benefits organisations and the NHS Business Services Authority have your correct name and address. If these details are not kept up to date you may be sent a Penalty Charge Notice, even if you are entitled to free prescriptions or dental treatment.

  • You ticked the wrong box
    If you tick the wrong box on your prescription or dental declaration form, your claim won’t match your exemption details. If they don’t match, you are at risk of getting a Penalty Charge Notice.

    When you complete the back of a prescription form or dental declaration form, it is your responsibility to make sure you tick the correct box

    If someone is collecting prescriptions on your behalf, make sure they know to tick the correct box on the back of the prescription. If dental staff are completing your dental form for you to sign, make sure they have ticked the right box for your eligibility.

You think you have an exceptional reason not to pay

A penalty charge can only be reviewed in certain circumstances. To make a challenge you will need to explain your circumstances to the NHS Business Services Authority (NHSBSA).

If you can show that you did not act wrongfully or with any lack of care, or you have an exceptional reason as to why you submitted an incorrect claim, the penalty charge may be dropped. However you will still need to pay the prescription charge.

The following reasons are not accepted:

  • you thought your exemption certificate was still valid, but it had expired
  • you feel you were misadvised by staff at your GP surgery, dental practice or pharmacy
  • you thought your prescription prepayment certificate would automatically renew

What to do if you’re not entitled to challenge

If you were not entitled to claim free NHS prescriptions or dental treatment between the dates on your Penalty Charge Notice, you must pay the charges listed on your letter.

Don’t delay – it is important to start paying your Penalty Charge Notice as soon as possible. Delaying payment could lead to an extra fine of up to £50.

You can pay off your Penalty Charge Notice in full so that the debt is cleared straight away, or you can pay it off in instalments.

Help towards your NHS health costs

If you get regular prescriptions or you need help managing the cost of your NHS health costs, the following options are available:

  • NHS exemption certificates – for maternity, medical and tax credit exemption
  • NHS Low Income Scheme
  • NHS prescription prepayment certificates

Use our online eligibility checker to find out whether you can claim free or reduced NHS prescriptions or dental treatment.
